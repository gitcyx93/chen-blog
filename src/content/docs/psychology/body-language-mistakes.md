## The Importance of [[hand gestures]]

- We understand **competence** in two ways. Very highly [[Competence|competent]] people know their content so well that they can speak to you on two tracks. They can communicate verbally, but they also convey meaning through their hands.
    
    我们以两种方式理解 **能力**。非常 **有能力** 的人对他们的内容了如指掌，以至于他们可以在 **两个层面** 上与人交流——他们不仅可以用 **语言表达**，还可以通过 **手势** 传达信息。
    
- This is why we loved **picture books** as kids. When someone is speaking, we're not only listening to their words, but also observing whether their hands are outlining their words.
    
    这就是为什么我们小时候喜欢 **图画书**。当有人讲话时，我们不仅在听他们的 **语言**，还在观察他们的 **手势** 是否在描绘他们所说的内容。
    

## [[vocal variety]] is a gift


- [[Vocal variety|声音变化]] is a **gift** to your listener that makes you more engaging. It allows them to recognize different segments of your speech—"Oh, we’re doing a number now," "Oh, we have a story now," or "This is an important point, I better write it down."
    
    **声音变化** 是给听众的 **礼物**，它让你更具 **吸引力**。它能帮助听众快速识别你的演讲内容，比如：“哦，我们现在在 **列举数字**”、“哦，这是一个 **故事**”、“哦，这是个 **重要观点**，我得记下来”。
    
- Really good **TED speakers** are outlining their talk for you. Essentially, they are helping to reduce your [[cognitive load|认知负荷]].
    
    优秀的 **TED 演讲者** 会为你勾勒他们的 **演讲结构**。本质上，他们是在帮助你减少 [[cognitive load|认知负荷]] 损耗。
    

### [[the perfect distance]] for conversation

- The **perfect distance** between two people having a good conversation is that we could shake hands if we wanted to.
    
    进行良好对话的 **完美距离** 是两个人可以随时 **握手** 的范围。
    
- There are **four different [[Space zones]]**, and these are really good to know if you're trying to set yourself up for success:
    
    **人际交往中有四个不同的空间区域**，如果你想让自己处于 **有利位置**，了解这些区域很有帮助：
    
    - **Public Zone（公共区域）**: About **5 to 8 feet away** (**大约 1.5 到 2.5 米**)
    - **Social Zone（社交区域）**: Depending on who you ask, about **3 to 5 feet away** (**大约 0.9 到 1.5 米**)
    - **Personal Zone（个人区域）**: Our **favorite zone**—about **an arm's distance apart** (**大约手臂的长度**)
    - **Intimate Zone（亲密区域）**: **Closer than arm’s length** (**比手臂更近**)
